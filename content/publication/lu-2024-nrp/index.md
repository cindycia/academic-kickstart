abstract: "Recent advances in robotics have enabled robots with increasingly higher degrees of freedom (DOFs), such as legged robots and humanoids. As a result, there is a growing need for motion planners that can effectively handle robots with whole-body articulation and large workspaces. Neural Randomized Planning (NRP) presents a learning-enhanced sampling-based motion planner, integrating neural sampling distributions with randomized search to scale up planning for high-DOF whole-body robot motion. NRP exploits both global and local environment representations during planning, learns from previously-planned environments, and generalizes to novel scenes. Experiments in challenging simulation tasks (8D-Snake and 11D-Fetch) and a real-world household environment demonstrate that NRP significantly improves planning success rate and path optimality over classical and learning-based baselines, while exhibiting strong zero-shot generalization."
